The transformation

Bare frame to beautiful — by hand.

Every plank of cedar fitted by hand. River stone laid across the floor. A Nippa wood stove that throws serious heat. Multi-tier benches built for lying flat, sitting up, or sweating it out with people you love. The outside was always part of the joke. The inside was always the point.

What you're getting into

Heat. Cold. Feast.

The Sauna
Wood-fired. Cedar-lined. River stone floors.

A Nippa wood stove, cedar benches on three tiers, and windows that let the light in without breaking the heat. Seats up to 8 at a time — rotate through for parties of 30 or more over a 5-hour session.

The Cold Plunge
A wine barrel full of cold water waiting for you.

Step out of the sauna and straight into the barrel. Hot to cold, over and over. The contrast is the whole point. Your body will thank you the next morning.

The Community
The kind of night people talk about for years.

This isn't a spa. It's not a wellness retreat. It's a private experience built around fire, cold water, good food, and the people you bring with you. The Russians figured this out centuries ago — sauna is about community as much as heat. We lean into that fully.

The Fire Master
Every session is managed, start to finish.

Your host manages the fire from first light to last log. This isn't self-serve — it's a curated experience. The Nippa takes time and attention to run right, and that's part of what you're paying for.

The Feast
Borscht. Dark bread. Good company.

The Russian tradition is heat, then eat. Veniks, borscht, cold drinks, dark bread, people lingering around a table after an hour in the sauna. Come ready to stay. Leave feeling like you've been somewhere real.
